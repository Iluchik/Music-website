Pendulum

Pendulum is an Australian drum and bass band formed in 2002 in Perth by Rob Swire, Gareth McGrillen and Paul Harding.

Swire and McGrillen were members of the metal band Oxygen, but after listening to Konflict's performance, they were inspired by drum and bass. They founded the band Pendulum together with Harding, a DJ familiar with the drum and bass scene. In 2003, the band moved to the UK.

Although the album Hold Your Colour was designed in the genre of drum and bass, later Pendulum began to experiment with a musical style, which was reflected in the albums In Silico and Immersion, where elements of alternative rock and heavy metal, dubstep, industrial, hard rock and house appeared.

Nero

Nero is a British electronic duo playing in the styles of electro-house, techno, drum and bass and dubstep. Originated in London, it consists of Dan Stevens and Joe Ray. Alana Watson performs lead vocals on many of the duo's tracks.

Ray and Stevens were born in 1984. Ray grew up in Northwood, London, while Stevens grew up in Notting Hill. The boys met Watson when they turned 18. Ray played acoustic guitar, and Stevens played cello with the support of his father, who was a jazz musician. At the age of 17, Stevens attended a specialized music school in Pimlico. Outside of school, both friends made electronic music on their home computers. They first started working together at the age of 17, setting up a studio in Stevens' bedroom.

Nero's debut release was "Space 2001" as part of the three-layer vinyl record "Straight Outta Leicester" on Reformed Recordings in 2004. Watson began to participate in the recording of Nero tracks since 2008, namely with the tracks "Solid Air" and "This Way", which were their debut compositions in the dubstep genre.

The Prodigy

The Prodigy is a British musical group formed in 1990 and focused on electronic music. Along with Fatboy Slim, The Chemical Brothers and The Crystal Method, The Prodigy are pioneers of the bigbit genre, which gained popularity in the 1990s and 2000s.

The band appeared on the underground rave scene in the early 1990s and since then has been very popular and famous all over the world. In total, more than 25 million copies of the band's albums have been sold worldwide. As of November 2018, The Prodigy's total album sales in the UK are 4,707,982 copies.

The Prodigy were able to create their own unique sound using elements of various styles in music, ranging from hardcore techno, industrial and breakbeat in the early 1990s and ending with electro-rock with vocal elements of punk at a later time.

Gydra

The Gydra project was originally a trio founded in 2014, later transformed into a duo consisting of the legend of the Russian drum and bass scene, the host of the Neuropunk podcast and the founder of the record label there Evgeny Besman aka bes and Moscow producer Nikolai Shilov (aka Menfort).

Hydra is one of the most impressive and rapidly developing electronic projects in Russia. The guys' tracks sound in sets and podcasts of world-famous DJs: Noisia, Black Sun Empire, Audio, Ed Rush & Optical, Prolix, BTK, Optiv, Bad Company, Jade, Billain, DC Breaks and others.

During its entire existence, the band has managed to release countless releases on the world's leading dnb imprints, such as Eatbrain, Blackout, RAM, Bad Taste, Renegade Hardware, Cause 4 Concern and others, and many of the released tracks have repeatedly been included in Beatport Drum&Bass Top 10.

Current Value

Tim Eliot, known under the creative pseudonym Current Value, is a German musician who writes music in the genre of drum and bass.

Tim Eliot studied piano as a child. In the early 1990s, he began experimenting with electronic music. In 1998, his first works were released, including several singles and a full-length album Frequency Hunt, designed in the style of drum and bass. In the following years, he released several more records. Since 2002, the influence of techno music has been felt in his work, his compositions have become more melodic. Eliot released his solo albums under the pseudonym Current Value.

In addition to his own albums, Eliot participated in several joint projects. Together with Dean Rodell, in early 2010, he founded the band Machine Code, which performed music in the style of dubstep, industrial and techno.
